Beginner's DotA League


Rules
Message any of the staff on discord with any concerns.
All concerns/reports will be reviewed on a case by case basis by staff.
Please work with the staff and be understanding of the process in all cases.
1.x: Discord Use
1.1: Discord, and in-game DotA "pro names" should be similar enough to recognize that they belong to the same user. Your DotA pro name appears in league games while retaining your Steam name in pubs. To edit your pro name, view your profile in-game, click the friends icon in the bottom left, and click on the word "teams." There you can edit your pro-name. The name should be the same, or as similar as possible, to your Discord name.
1.2: Regional text channels are both used for parties and general discussion, but parties get priority. Refrain from bloating the chat if parties need to use it to communicate for any reason.
1.3: Regional pings allowed only in LFG channels and aren't permitted within 20 minutes of last ping (check the timestamps). If starting a different party within this time, DO NOT PING. Just post your LFG message.
1.4: In house channels only used for (obviously) in house leagues and scrims
2.x: Behavior
2.1: NO DISCUSSION OF POLITICS. We're here because we enjoy DOTA. Accept that there are others you disagree with on major political opinion. Any reference to politics should be in jest, and received in jest, and never to express serious political opinion (E.g. Use of :TrumpApproves).
2.2: Respect others' opinions and differences; we have members all across the world. If somebody expresses concern about being offended, cease whatever behavior/discussion is causing it. There will be no use of the defense: "you shouldn't be offended." Overly offensive posts will get deleted and both parties spoken to for follow-up. Also, do not attack someone if they were unknowingly offensive; some people truly do not understand when they may be being offensive. Both parties should make attempts to remedy any discomfort or situations. WHEN IN DOUBT, CONTACT A STAFF MEMBER.
2.3: Racism, sexism, homophobia, etc. are reportable offenses. If someone asks you to refrain from using certain words/slurs they find offensive, respect their wishes, even if you disagree. SARCASM IS NOT AN EXCUSE FOR BEING OFFENSIVE. Please think how your sarcastic humor may be received by others before posting it. Try and avoid combining sensitive topics and sarcasm!
2.4: In-game and Discord banter is permitted in moderation but should be ceased immediately if any player expresses discomfort. This includes banter between teams; do not be sore winner or sore losers. Keep persistent arguments between PMs and out of Discord/in-game channels.
2.5: Use < > tags around links that (even) may be considered NSFW/NSFL/Offensive. Please think before sharing any links.
3.x: League Rules
3.1: Smurf accounts not allowed. DOTA2 profile must be public. Highest allowed rank for official league participation is Legend 5. Upon reaching Ancient rank, players are automatically given "Coach" role, and can no longer participate in weekend in-house matches. They are welcome to stay in the community to party up for pubs, and participate in unofficial in-houses (whole lobby must agree to it and non-coach roles get priority in queue regardless of time or matches played). When DOTA ranks reset, players are reconsidered.
3.2: Players must maintain good-faith effort to win/coordinate at all times. Any reportable behaviors in DOTA2 are also reportable in the league (e.g. intentional feeding, griefing, AFK, communication abuse, etc.). No fake/joke "GG" mid-game. "GG" forfeit mechanic is only to be used to end games early. Teams should respectfully continue to play in good faith if even just one player disagrees to the forfeit.
3.3: Players must join assigned voice channel prior to hero draft, regardless of their ability to use voice comm. Players must make attempt to communicate (e.g. in game chat, chat-wheel, etc). Disregarding all team comms and not having a good-faith effort to win is actionable.
3.4: Each team is gets 5 minutes total pause time but teams may agree to stay paused, if necessary.
3.5: Dodging games will be disciplined with varying queue ban times.
3.6: No arguing over roles and respect other team members' opinions. If unresolvable, higher "/roll" gets priority (this is ONLY in the case that even members' can't help come to a solution).
3.7: Money prize pool will vary with sponsorship.
3.8: Directions to organize games:
1) Official league games are around the clock on Fri-Sun. There is no fixed schedule for matches. Follow directions below to begin a match.
2) Players queue for a game by entering their region's In-House "Queue" voice channel until ten players are present. The two players with highest rank on leaderboard (link below) are the captains.
3) Captains can either draft players one by one, or use the google doc (link below) for a balanced shuffle.
4) Ping your regions admins @na_admin/@sea_admin/@eu_admin in discord to have them create the private DOTA2 lobby (will be password protected). (Ping specific admins from other regions if necessary.) Separate into your team's voice channels in Discord.
5) After the match completes, winning team's captain is responsible to post the match ID in their region's In-House #MatchResults channel. (It will be created soon)
4.x: Discipline
4.1: All incidents will be discussed between staffs and any parties involved (individually or together) and actions taken are at the discretion of staffs. If necessary, an announcement regarding the incident will be made to the server (i.e. to set precedents or reiterate rules).
4.2: All disciplinary action (and severity) will be taken with discretion of staff as as a group, but will generally follow this stepwise fashion:
1st) Warning
2nd) 1 Day Ban
3rd) 1 Week Ban
4th) Permanent Ban
